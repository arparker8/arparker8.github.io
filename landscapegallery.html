My Favorite Landscape Photos

The photos in this gallery contain some of my favorite landscape photos that I have taken over the last 6 years. Landscape or nature photos are my favorite photos to take. I have probably a thousand different ones but when going through my photos these were the ones that stood out to me the most.

"Stormy Sky" - June 15th, 2023

This photo I took on a whim when there was a storm heading toward my house. Once I took the photo, I loved the contrast in colors. From the rich green to the dark blue in the distance from the storm. The photo wasn’t flat and told a story. The contrast also helped make the formation of the clouds visible.

"Beautiful Sunset" - December 25th, 2022

Sunsets are my favorite time of day; I have probably hundreds of photos of them. It was hard to pick just one but this one stood out to me. I loved the cloud formation as well as the colors that are being highlighted in the sky.

"Garden of the Gods 1" - July 9th, 2022

"Garden of the Gods 2" - July 9th, 2022

These two photos are ones I took when I took a day trip up to Colorado Springs to go see the Garden of the Gods. It was a beautiful sunny day but there were some clouds, which is how I got the photo on the left. I have found that contrast in color can really make a photo more dynamic and outdoors photos have so many different rich colors.

"Capturing Lightning 1" - August 31st, 2018

"Capturing Lightning 2" - August 31st, 2018

I am very happy with these lightning photos they are one of my favorites of all time. The reasoning is because getting lightning photos is super hard, I found out. It’s all about timing and I feel like it’s lucky because you never know when or where it’s going to strike. So, you hope that you click at the right time and that the subject is in focus. It was a lot of fun though testing and trying again, I learn a lot about my camera taking these photos.

"Night Shot 1" - July 16th, 2019

"Night shot 2" - November 23rd, 2018

These last two photos are my attempts at night photography. I love looking at the night sky and where I live you can see the stars very clearly because we don’t live near a city or town. I wanted to try and capture the moon and stars with my camera. You must have your camera on a tripod and the shutter needs to be open for a longer amount of time because of how little lighting there is at night. I enjoyed taking these photos and will definitely be taking more night photos in the future.
